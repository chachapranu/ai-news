# AI News Digest - March 10, 2026

## 🔐 Security & Threats

### OpenAI Launches Codex Security Agent
OpenAI began rolling out **Codex Security**, an AI-powered security agent designed to find, validate, and propose fixes for vulnerabilities. In its beta phase over the last 30 days, Codex Security scanned more than 1.2 million commits across external repositories, identifying 792 critical findings and 10,561 high-severity issues.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/openai-codex-security-scanned-12.html)

### AI Assistants Exploited as C2 Relays
Cybersecurity researchers disclosed that AI assistants with web browsing or URL fetching capabilities can be turned into stealthy command-and-control (C2) relays. The technique has been demonstrated against **Microsoft Copilot** and **xAI Grok**, raising concerns about AI security vulnerabilities.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/researchers-show-copilot-and-grok-can.html)

### Anthropic Discovers Firefox Vulnerabilities
Anthropic discovered 22 new security vulnerabilities in the **Firefox web browser** as part of a security partnership with Mozilla: 14 classified as high severity, seven as moderate, and one as low.

**Source:** General AI news coverage

### AI Companies Target Congressional Candidate
AI companies are spending millions against former Palantir employee **Alex Bores**, who quit over the company's ICE work and is now running for New York's 12th congressional district. Super PAC "Leading the Future," backed by Palantir co-founder Joe Lonsdale, OpenAI President Greg Brockman, and Andreessen Horowitz, has raised **$125 million** to target candidates introducing AI legislation.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/03/ai-companies-are-spending-millions-to-thwart-this-former-tech-execs-congressional-bid/)

### Anthropic's Model Context Protocol Becomes Standard
Anthropic's **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**, described as "USB-C for AI," is quickly becoming the industry standard for AI agent interoperability. OpenAI and Microsoft have publicly embraced MCP, and Anthropic recently donated it to the Linux Foundation's new Agentic AI Foundation.

**Source:** [TechCrunch AI Predictions](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/02/in-2026-ai-will-move-from-hype-to-pragmatism/)
