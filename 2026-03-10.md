# AI News Digest - March 10, 2026

## 🔐 Security & Threats

### OpenAI Launches Codex Security Agent
OpenAI began rolling out **Codex Security**, an AI-powered security agent designed to find, validate, and propose fixes for vulnerabilities. Available in research preview to ChatGPT Pro, Enterprise, Business, and Edu customers, the tool has scanned more than 1.2 million commits across external repositories in the last 30 days, identifying 792 critical findings and 10,561 high-severity issues.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/openai-codex-security-scanned-12.html) | [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/openai-releases-ai-agent-security-tool-for-research-preview)

### AI Assistants Exploited as C2 Relays
Cybersecurity researchers disclosed that AI assistants with web browsing or URL fetching capabilities can be turned into stealthy command-and-control (C2) relays. The technique has been demonstrated against **Microsoft Copilot** and **xAI Grok**, raising concerns about AI security vulnerabilities.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/researchers-show-copilot-and-grok-can.html)

### Anthropic Discovers 22 Firefox Vulnerabilities
Anthropic discovered 22 new security vulnerabilities in the **Firefox web browser** as part of a security partnership with Mozilla: 14 classified as high severity, seven as moderate, and one as low. The issues have been addressed in Firefox 148.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/search/label/artificial%20intelligence)

### Model Context Protocol: AI's Fastest-Growing Attack Surface
The Model Context Protocol (MCP) has emerged as a major security concern, with **30 CVEs filed in just 60 days**. Research reveals that **38% of 500+ scanned MCP servers completely lack authentication**. Additionally, security researchers analyzed more than 30,000 skills and found that **over a quarter contained at least one vulnerability**. Research also found that adding around 250 poisoned documents to training data can embed hidden triggers inside a model without affecting how it performs in normal testing.

**Source:** [Adversa AI](https://adversa.ai/blog/top-mcp-security-resources-march-2026/)

### IBM X-Force: 44% Increase in AI-Driven Attacks
IBM's 2026 X-Force Threat Index reports a **44% increase in attacks** beginning with exploitation of public-facing applications, largely driven by missing authentication controls and AI-enabled vulnerability discovery. The report highlights that while **83% of organizations plan to deploy agentic AI**, only **29% are ready to operate those systems securely**.

**Source:** [IBM Newsroom](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-02-25-ibm-2026-x-force-threat-index-ai-driven-attacks-are-escalating-as-basic-security-gaps-leave-enterprises-exposed)

### APT28 Surveillance Campaign Targets Ukraine
Russian state-sponsored hacking group APT28 has been observed using implants dubbed BEARDSHELL and COVENANT to facilitate long-term surveillance of Ukrainian military personnel since April 2024.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/)

## 📊 Trends & Analysis

### 2026: From Hype to Pragmatism
Industry analysts report that if 2025 was the year AI got a "vibe check," **2026 will be the year the tech gets practical**. Focus is shifting away from building ever-larger language models and toward making AI genuinely usable in real-world applications.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/02/in-2026-ai-will-move-from-hype-to-pragmatism/)

### World Models Emerge as Major AI Trend
World models are becoming a significant focus in AI development, with 2026 shaping up to be a breakthrough year for this technology. World models learn abstract representations of real-world sensor data, ignore unpredictable details, and make predictions in representation space, allowing agentic systems to predict consequences of actions and plan task sequences. This represents a shift from pure language models to systems that can understand and reason about physical reality.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/) | [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/22/1131661/yann-lecuns-new-venture-ami-labs/)

### Pro-Human AI Declaration Released
A bipartisan coalition has assembled the **Pro-Human AI Declaration**, a framework for responsible AI development. The declaration, signed by hundreds of experts and former officials, outlines five key pillars: keeping humans in charge, avoiding power concentration, protecting human experience, preserving individual liberty, and holding AI companies legally accountable. MIT physicist Max Tegmark noted that polling shows **95% of Americans oppose an unregulated race to superintelligence**.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/a-roadmap-for-ai-if-anyone-will-listen/)

## 🚀 Product Launches

### Yann LeCun's AMI Labs: World Models Over LLMs
Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI), founded by **Yann LeCun** (former Meta chief AI scientist and Turing Award winner), is developing world models that learn abstract representations of reality. The Paris-based company is building AI architectures capable of persistent memory, planning, and controllable decision-making for healthcare, robotics, and industrial applications.

AMI's approach uses **JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture)**, which trains AI models to understand the world by learning underlying rules from observation, similar to how a baby learns about gravity. This is the foundation for common sense and the key to building truly intelligent systems that can reason and plan in the real world.

LeCun has been a vocal critic of the industry's focus on large language models, calling them **"statistical pattern-matchers dressed up as intelligence"** that are incapable of genuine reasoning, planning, or understanding physical reality. AMI's first partnership is with clinical AI developer Nabla.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/yann-lecuns-ami-labs-raises-1-03-billion-to-build-world-models/) | [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/22/1131661/yann-lecuns-new-venture-ami-labs/) | [Business Today](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/news/story/former-meta-ai-chief-yann-lecuns-startup-raises-103-billion-to-build-world-models-519856-2026-03-10)
