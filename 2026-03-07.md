### CyberStrikeAI Used in FortiGate Attacks
A sophisticated threat actor leveraged **CyberStrikeAI**, an open-source AI-native security testing platform, to compromise over **600 Fortinet FortiGate devices across 55 countries** between January 20 and February 26, 2026. The attacks utilized commercial generative AI services like Anthropic Claude and DeepSeek to execute sophisticated exploits.

**Key Details:**
- **The Tool:** CyberStrikeAI is a Go-based framework integrating "more than 100 security tools to enable vulnerability discovery, attack-chain analysis" and other offensive capabilities
- **Infrastructure:** 21 unique IP addresses detected running CyberStrikeAI, with servers primarily in China, Singapore, and Hong Kong, plus additional infrastructure in the U.S., Japan, and Switzerland
- **Attribution:** The tool is maintained by a Chinese developer using the alias Ed1s0nZ on GitHub. Security researchers assess the developer has "ties to the Chinese government," with interactions involving Knownsec 404, a Chinese security firm exposed in data breaches revealing connections to state-sponsored cyber operations and the Ministry of State Security

This represents a concerning evolution in the proliferation of AI-augmented offensive security tools, indicating threat actors are increasingly leveraging AI to automate and scale cyberattack campaigns against critical infrastructure worldwide.

- **Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/open-source-cyberstrikeai-deployed-in.html)

### Meta Faces Class Action Lawsuit Over AI Smart Glasses Privacy
Meta is facing a class action lawsuit over its AI smart glasses after an investigation by Swedish newspapers found that workers at a Kenya-based subcontractor (Sama in Nairobi) are reviewing footage from customers' glasses, which included sensitive content like nudity, people having sex, and using the toilet.

**The Lawsuit:**
Filed Wednesday in federal court in San Francisco by plaintiffs Gina Bartone (New Jersey) and Mateo Canu (California), represented by the Clarkson Law Firm. The suit alleges:
- Meta violated privacy laws and engaged in false advertising
- Meta and Luxottica of America's conduct violates consumer protection laws
- No reasonable consumer would understand "designed for privacy, controlled by you" or "built for your privacy" to mean deeply personal footage would be viewed by human workers overseas

**The Investigation:**
Swedish newspapers Svenska Dagbladet and Göteborgs-Posten identified the Nairobi-based data subcontractor as Sama, which employs workers to train AI systems by manually annotating videos, images, and speech for Meta's AI services.

**Regulatory Response:**
The U.K.'s Information Commissioner's Office has launched an investigation into the matter.

**Meta's Response:**
Meta told the BBC that when people share content with Meta AI, it uses contractors to review the information to improve the glasses experience, which is explained in its privacy policy.

## 📈 Key Takeaways

1. **Security is the Critical Challenge:** As AI capabilities accelerate, security vulnerabilities are multiplying faster than defenses can adapt. The MCP security crisis (30 CVEs in 60 days), rising breakout speeds (29 minutes average), and Claude finding 22 Firefox bugs all demonstrate the urgent need for better AI security frameworks.

2. **Massive Capital Consolidation:** The record-breaking funding rounds for OpenAI ($110B) and Anthropic ($30B) suggest the AI industry is consolidating around a few well-capitalized players, potentially creating higher barriers to entry for new competitors.

3. **Enterprise Adoption Accelerating:** Despite controversies like the Anthropic-Pentagon dispute, enterprise adoption continues to accelerate as major cloud providers (Microsoft, Google, AWS) ensure AI services remain available to business customers.

4. **From Hype to Implementation:** Industry consensus suggests 2026 marks the transition from AI experimentation to practical deployment, with focus shifting to world models, agentic systems, and vertical-specific solutions.

5. **Regulatory Scrutiny Intensifying:** The Pentagon's unprecedented designation of a US AI company (Anthropic) as a supply-chain risk signals that AI governance and regulation will be major themes in 2026.

6. **AI Security Paradox:** AI tools are simultaneously discovering vulnerabilities (like Claude finding 22 Firefox bugs) while also creating new attack vectors (MCP vulnerabilities, prompt injection, etc.).

7. **Investment Focus Narrowing:** VCs are moving away from generic AI tools toward vertical-specific solutions with proprietary data and deep workflow integration.

8. **The coding assistant market is exploding**, with Cursor's rapid ARR growth ($2B) and new product launches demonstrating enterprise appetite for AI developer tools.
