## 🔐 Security & Threats

### CyberStrikeAI Deployed in FortiGate Attacks Across 55 Countries

A sophisticated AI-powered cyberattack campaign leveraged CyberStrikeAI, an open-source security testing platform, to compromise over 600 Fortinet FortiGate devices across 55 countries between January 11 and February 18, 2026.

*Key Details:*
- **What is CyberStrikeAI?** An open-source offensive security tool developed in Go by Chinese developer Ed1s0nZ, integrating over 100 security tools for vulnerability discovery, attack-chain analysis, and automated exploitation
- **Attack Method:** Russian-speaking threat actors used commercial generative AI services (including Anthropic Claude and DeepSeek) for automated scanning and exploitation
- **Targets:** FortiGate appliances with exposed management ports and weak credentials
- **Developer Concerns:** Researchers assess Ed1s0nZ has connections to the Chinese government and has interacted with Knownsec 404, a firm with alleged ties to state-sponsored cyber operations

Team Cymru detected 21 unique IP addresses running CyberStrikeAI between January 20 and February 26, 2026, primarily hosted in China, Singapore, and Hong Kong.

### NIST Seeks Public Comment on AI Agent Security

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) is requesting public input on AI agent security with a deadline of March 9, 2026, focusing on autonomous AI systems that can take real-world actions.

*Novel Threats Identified:*
- **Prompt injection and behavioral hijacking** attacks targeting agent decision-making
- **Cascade failures** from multi-agent coordination breakdowns
- **Agent registration/tracking** requirements (similar to drone registration systems)

Security experts emphasized that AI agents behave more like "employees to be supervised" than certified software, requiring:
- Runtime behavioral monitoring rather than pre-deployment certification alone
- Blast radius containment through sandboxing and permission scoping
- Empirical benchmarking of agent vulnerabilities against real systems

**Deadline:** March 9, 2026, 11:59 PM ET
**Submission Portal:** regulations.gov/commenton/NIST-2025-0035-0001

**Source:** [Hacker News Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47131689)

## 💼 Enterprise & Business

### AI Companies Spend $125M to Block Pro-Regulation Candidates

A tech billionaire-backed super PAC called "Leading the Future" is spending $125 million to oppose congressional candidates pushing for AI regulation, marking one of the largest political campaigns targeting tech policy lawmakers.

*Campaign Details:*
- **Primary Target:** Alex Bores, New York congressional candidate and former tech executive who sponsored the RAISE Act
- **Key Backers:** Palantir co-founder Joe Lonsdale, OpenAI President Greg Brockman, and VC firm Andreessen Horowitz
- **The Trigger:** Bores sponsored the RAISE Act, an AI transparency bill signed into law in December requiring large AI labs with $500M+ revenue to publish public safety plans
- **PAC Strategy:** Support candidates with "light-to-no-touch" approach to AI regulation while opposing those introducing AI legislation

This represents a significant clash between pro-regulation advocates and industry figures seeking to minimize regulatory constraints on AI development.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/03/ai-companies-are-spending-millions-to-thwart-this-former-tech-execs-congressional-bid/)

### What Investors No Longer Want in AI SaaS Companies

Venture capitalists are shifting investment priorities, moving away from generic AI wrappers toward deeper, more defensible businesses.

*No Longer Attractive:*
- Thin workflow layers and generic horizontal tools
- Light product management and surface-level analytics
- Generic vertical software without proprietary data moats
- Products whose differentiation lives mostly in UI and automation
- Basic CRM clones and thin AI wrappers built on top of existing APIs
- Workflow automation tools that coordinate human work (as AI agents replace humans)
- Integration layers (as Anthropic's Model Context Protocol makes connections easier)

*What VCs Want Now:*
- **AI-native infrastructure** with deep technical moats
- **Vertical SaaS with proprietary data** and domain expertise
- **Systems of action** helping users complete tasks
- **Platforms deeply embedded** in mission-critical workflows
- **Workflow ownership** rather than just task execution
- **Real product depth** beyond UI layers
- **Consumption-based pricing models** over rigid per-seat pricing

**Key Insight from Jake Saper (Emergence Capital):** "Pre-Claude, getting humans to do their jobs inside your software was a powerful moat, but if agents are doing the work, who cares about human workflow?"

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/01/investors-spill-what-they-arent-looking-for-anymore-in-ai-saas-companies/)

## 🚀 Product Launches

### CollectivIQ: Multi-Model AI Answer Platform

A new startup called CollectivIQ is taking a novel approach to improving AI answer reliability by crowdsourcing responses from multiple AI models simultaneously.

*How It Works:*
- Queries ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Grok, and up to 10 additional models at once
- Presents users with diverse outputs side-by-side for comparison
- Aims to improve accuracy through consensus and diverse perspectives
- Started rolling out internally to employees in early 2026

*The Pitch:* Rather than relying on a single AI model's response, CollectivIQ helps users evaluate reliability by comparing how different systems address the same question.

## 💰 Funding & Investment

### Investment Trends: Quality Over Quantity

Investors are reallocating capital toward businesses that own workflows, data, and domain expertise—and away from products that can be easily replicated. According to Igor Ryabenkiy (AltaIR Capital): "If the product is mostly an interface layer without deep integration, proprietary data, or embedded process knowledge, strong AI-native teams can rebuild it quickly."

Key investment criteria for 2026:
- Proprietary data moats
- Deep workflow integration
- Domain expertise and specialized knowledge
- Fast adaptation capabilities
- Flexible, consumption-based pricing models

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/01/investors-spill-what-they-arent-looking-for-anymore-in-ai-saas-companies/)

## 📈 Key Takeaways

1. **Security is Critical:** AI-powered cyberattacks are becoming more sophisticated, with tools like CyberStrikeAI enabling automated exploitation at scale. Organizations must implement runtime behavioral monitoring for AI agents.

2. **Regulatory Battles Heating Up:** The AI industry is spending massive amounts ($125M+) on political campaigns to oppose regulation, indicating how high the stakes have become for policy decisions.

3. **The Agent Era Has Arrived:** VCs are fundamentally rethinking what makes a valuable software company as AI agents replace human workflows. Products that simply coordinate human work are losing favor.

4. **Moats Must Be Deeper:** Generic AI wrappers and thin UI layers are no longer defensible. Companies need proprietary data, deep workflow ownership, and embedded domain expertise to attract investment.

5. **Consolidation Accelerating:** The market is shifting toward fewer, more capable vendors with consumption-based models rather than per-seat pricing. Integration layers are becoming commoditized.

6. **Multi-Model Approaches Emerging:** New startups like CollectivIQ are exploring consensus-based approaches using multiple AI models to improve reliability and accuracy.

7. **Government Oversight Coming:** NIST's request for comment on AI agent security signals that formal government frameworks for autonomous AI systems are in development.
