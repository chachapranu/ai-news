### Hacker Exploits Anthropic's Claude to Steal Mexican Government Data
A hacker used Anthropic's Claude AI chatbot to carry out cyberattacks against Mexican government agencies, successfully stealing sensitive tax and voter information. This incident highlights growing concerns about AI tools being weaponized for malicious purposes.

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-25/hacker-used-anthropic-s-claude-to-steal-sensitive-mexican-data)

### AI-Assisted Attack Compromises 600+ FortiGate Devices Globally
A Russian-speaking, financially motivated threat actor leveraged commercial generative AI services to compromise over 600 FortiGate devices across 55 countries between January 11 and February 18, 2026. The campaign exploited exposed management ports and weak credentials with single-factor authentication, demonstrating how AI enables unsophisticated actors to execute large-scale attacks.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/ai-assisted-threat-actor-compromises.html)

### North Korean Hackers Using Google Gemini for Reconnaissance
Google reported that UNC2970, a North Korea-linked threat actor, has been using its Gemini AI model to conduct reconnaissance on targets. The group leveraged Gemini to synthesize open-source intelligence (OSINT) and profile high-value targets for campaign planning.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/google-reports-state-backed-hackers.html)

### Infostealer Malware Targets AI Agent Credentials
Security researchers at Hudson Rock discovered that infostealer malware has evolved to steal OpenClaw AI agent configuration files and gateway tokens, marking a significant milestone in cybercriminal behavior. This development came shortly before OpenAI CEO Sam Altman announced on February 15 that OpenClaw's founder would be joining OpenAI.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/infostealer-steals-openclaw-ai-agent.html)

### AI Collapses Security Response Windows
New analysis reveals that AI-driven threats are adapting in real time, dramatically reducing the window organizations have to respond to security incidents. This represents a fundamental shift in cybersecurity dynamics for 2026.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/from-exposure-to-exploitation-how-ai.html)

India became the world's largest market for generative AI app downloads in 2025, according to Sensor Tower, widening its lead over the U.S. as installs jumped 207% year-over-year.
