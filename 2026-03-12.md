### CyberStrikeAI Deployed in Massive FortiGate Campaign

An open-source AI-powered offensive security platform, built by a developer with documented ties to China's Ministry of State Security, has been linked to a campaign that **compromised over 600 FortiGate devices across 55 countries** in just five weeks (January 11 - February 18, 2026).

**Key Details:**
- The attacker was described as a Russian-speaking, financially motivated threat actor with "low-to-medium baseline technical capability, significantly augmented by AI"
- Team Cymru observed **21 unique IP addresses running CyberStrikeAI** between January 20 and February 26, 2026
- Servers were primarily hosted in China, Singapore, and Hong Kong
- By February 26, multiple new servers were being stood up daily — with five unique IPs observed on that single date alone
- The platform integrates over 100 security tools with an AI decision engine compatible with GPT, Claude, and DeepSeek models

**State Connections:** In December 2025, the developer Ed1s0nZ submitted CyberStrikeAI to the Starlink Project managed by Knownsec 404, a private firm with known ties to the MSS and the Chinese People's Liberation Army. The developer recently scrubbed this reference from their profile as the tool gained notoriety.

### Meta Acquires Moltbook, the AI Agent Social Network

Meta acquired **Moltbook**, a viral social network designed for AI agents, on March 10, 2026. The deal brings Moltbook's creators — Matt Schlicht and Ben Parr — into Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL), run by former Scale AI CEO Alexandr Wang.

**What is Moltbook?**
- A social network where AI bots post, comment, and argue just like people
- Formatted similarly to Reddit
- Only for AI agents, which autonomously join after a human shares a sign-up link
- OpenClaw agents largely built the platform

**Strategic Vision:**
- Meta sees this as opening "new ways for AI agents to work for people and businesses"
- The deal could signal how Meta sees AI agents shaping future advertising and commerce on an "agentic web"
- Deal expected to close mid-March, with the team starting at MSL on March 16

## 💰 Funding & Investment

### Yann LeCun's AMI Labs Raises $1.03B for World Models

AMI Labs, the new venture co-founded by Turing Award winner **Yann LeCun** after he left Meta, has raised **$1.03 billion at a $3.5 billion pre-money valuation** in what is believed to be **the largest seed round ever raised by a European startup**.

**About World Models:**
- AMI is working on "world models," AI that learns from reality, not just from language
- The venture is developing AI architecture designed to learn predictive representations of environments through observation of the real world
- Based on JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture), proposed by LeCun in 2022

**Leadership and Investors:**
- CEO Alexandre LeBrun predicts that "In six months, every company will call itself a world model to raise funding"
- The round was co-led by Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, and Bezos Expeditions
- Participants include Nvidia Corp., Samsung Electronics, and angels including Tim and Rosemary Berners-Lee, Jim Breyer, Mark Cuban, Mark Leslie, Xavier Niel, and Eric Schmidt

## 📈 Key Takeaways

1. **Security is the New Battleground:** AI is being weaponized at scale (CyberStrikeAI), while companies rush to build AI security tools (Codex Security, Code Review, Promptfoo) to defend against the flood of AI-generated code and AI-powered attacks.

2. **Acquisitions Signal Strategic Shifts:** Major acquisitions (Moltbook, Forethought, InterPositive, Promptfoo) show tech giants positioning for an "agentic web" where AI agents autonomously interact, transact, and create.

3. **The Great Consolidation:** As predicted, 2026 is seeing enterprises spend more on AI through fewer vendors, with massive funding rounds going to proven platforms (Replit, Lovable, AMI Labs) rather than experimental startups.

4. **World Models vs. LLMs:** Yann LeCun's $1.03B raise for AMI Labs signals a major bet on world models (AI that learns from reality) as an alternative to language-model-based approaches, potentially reshaping the AI landscape.

5. **AI and Labor:** The conversation has shifted from "will AI replace jobs?" to companies openly admitting AI is the reason for layoffs, with hundreds of thousands of jobs cut and AI explicitly cited as the replacement.

6. **The Agentic Revolution:** From Moltbook (social networks for AI agents) to AgentMail (email for AI agents) to Zoom's AI avatars, infrastructure for autonomous AI agents is rapidly materializing — suggesting 2026 truly is the year agentic AI moves from demo to deployment.

7. **Trust Crisis:** The #QuitGPT movement, with 2.5 million users abandoning ChatGPT over the Pentagon deal, shows that AI companies face growing public scrutiny over ethics and military applications, potentially reshaping competitive dynamics.

8. **Hardware Goes Mainstream:** The $599 MacBook Neo and proliferation of AI glasses at MWC 2026 suggest AI-specific hardware is moving from enthusiast products to mass-market devices.
