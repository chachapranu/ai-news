# AI News Digest - March 9, 2026

## 🔐 Security & Threats

### OpenAI Codex Security Identifies 792 Critical Vulnerabilities
OpenAI launched Codex Security, an AI-powered security agent designed to find, validate, and propose fixes for vulnerabilities. Over the last 30 days, it scanned more than 1.2 million commits across external repositories, identifying 792 critical findings out of 10,561 high-severity issues total. The tool is available in research preview to ChatGPT Pro, Enterprise, Business, and Edu customers.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/openai-codex-security-scanned-12.html)

### AI-Powered Cyberattacks Target FortiGate Devices
Amazon Threat Intelligence detected an unknown attacker systematically targeting FortiGate devices using generative AI services like Anthropic Claude and DeepSeek. The attacker leveraged CyberStrikeAI, an open-source AI-native security testing platform, compromising over 600 appliances across 55 countries. This represents one of the first major instances of AI being weaponized for large-scale cyberattacks.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/open-source-cyberstrikeai-deployed-in.html)

### Anthropic Discovers 22 Firefox Vulnerabilities
In a recent security partnership with Mozilla, Anthropic's security team found 22 separate vulnerabilities in Firefox, with 14 classified as "high-severity". This collaboration demonstrates AI's potential for proactive security research.

**Source:** [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

### Chinese AI Labs Accused of Model Distillation
Anthropic alleged that DeepSeek, MiniMax, and Moonshot AI generated more than 16 million interactions with Claude using roughly 24,000 fraudulent accounts in an "industrial-scale campaign" to distill their model. OpenAI separately submitted a letter to U.S. legislators claiming similar distillation attempts by DeepSeek. This raises serious concerns about AI model theft and export controls.

**Sources:** [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-says-deepseek-moonshot-and-minimax-used-24-000-fake-accounts-to), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/24/anthropic-openai-china-firms-distillation-deepseek.html), [Startup News](https://startupnews.fyi/2026/03/09/anthropic-flags-large-scale-distillation-attempts-by-chinas-deepseek-minimax-and-moonshot/)

## 💼 Enterprise & Business

### Pentagon Designates Anthropic as "Supply Chain Risk"
Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the company refused to grant the Pentagon unlimited use of its technology. The designation effectively bars any company doing business with the Department of Defense from using Anthropic's technology, potentially wiping out dozens of major enterprise customers. Interestingly, DeepSeek—despite its ties to China—is not designated a supply chain risk, while OpenAI subsequently secured its own deal with the Defense Department.

**Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/a-roadmap-for-ai-if-anyone-will-listen/), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/04/trump-hegseth-anthropic-china-deepseek)

### Microsoft Unveils Coworkintegration Feature
Microsoft unveiled Coworkintegration, a new feature developed in close collaboration with Anthropic, designed to enhance Copilot capabilities. Details on specific functionality remain limited.

**Source:** [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

### OpenAI Releases Financial Services Tools
OpenAI released new financial services tools on March 5, rivaling Anthropic's offerings in the enterprise space. GPT-5.4 is now more capable of generating spreadsheets, documents, and presentations with less back-and-forth required from users.

**Sources:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/openai-releases-new-financial-services-tools-rivaling-anthropic), [MLQ AI](https://mlq.ai/prediction/brief/openai/openai-markets-brief-anthropic-leads-best-model-boards-as-gpt53-timing-locks-in-2026-03-07/)

### AI Governance Framework Gains Bipartisan Support
A bipartisan coalition assembled the Pro-Human Declaration, a framework for responsible AI development. Led by MIT physicist Max Tegmark, the initiative addresses the lack of coherent rules governing AI. Polling shows 95% of Americans oppose an unregulated race to superintelligence. The declaration focuses on five pillars: keeping humans in charge, avoiding power concentration, protecting human experience, preserving individual liberty, and holding AI companies legally accountable.

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/07/a-roadmap-for-ai-if-anyone-will-listen/)

1. **Security is Front and Center**: AI is simultaneously becoming a powerful security tool (Codex Security) and a weapon for attackers (CyberStrikeAI), creating an AI-powered arms race in cybersecurity.

2. **Geopolitical Tensions Intensify**: The Anthropic-Pentagon standoff and accusations of Chinese model distillation highlight growing geopolitical friction around AI capabilities and access.

3. **From Lab to Production**: 2026's defining theme is the shift from research breakthroughs to practical deployment, with investors demanding real business outcomes rather than impressive demos.

4. **Physical AI Emerges**: AI is moving beyond chatbots and generative tools into robotics, IoT devices, and physical systems that interact with the real world.

5. **Unprecedented Capital Flows**: With over $195B deployed in February alone and massive rounds continuing in March, AI has become the dominant force in technology investment—potentially creating sustainability concerns.

6. **Model Competition Heats Up**: March's wave of releases (GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and anticipated DeepSeek V4) shows fierce competition among frontier labs.

7. **Governance Gains Momentum**: Bipartisan support for the Pro-Human Declaration suggests 2026 may finally see coherent AI regulation, with 95% public support for oversight.

8. **Enterprise AI Matures**: Major corporations are moving beyond pilots to full-scale deployments, with AI expected to significantly impact the enterprise workforce this year.
