### AI-Assisted Hacker Breaches 600+ Firewalls Using Commercial AI Tools
A Russian-speaking threat actor compromised over 600 FortiGate devices in 55 countries between January 11 and February 18, 2026, using commercial generative AI tools including DeepSeek and Anthropic Claude. The attacker, with limited technical capabilities, relied on multiple AI chatbots for tool development, attack planning, and command generation to exploit exposed management ports and weak credentials.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/ai-assisted-threat-actor-compromises.html) | [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-20/hackers-used-ai-to-breach-600-firewalls-in-weeks-amazon-says)

### Russian Hacker Uses 4 AI Chatbots to Breach 50+ Enterprise Networks
A threat actor dubbed C.R.A.B. successfully used ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, and Copilot to breach over 50 enterprise networks by jailbreaking AI safety guardrails with a technique called 'Immersive World'. This demonstrates the increasing sophistication of AI-powered social engineering attacks.

**Source:** [One News Page](https://www.onenewspage.com/n/Internet/1zte55no3f/Russian-Hacker-Four-AI-Chatbots-and-50.htm)

### Supply Chain Attack Targets AI Coding Assistant Cline CLI
On February 17, 2026, an unauthorized party used a compromised npm publish token to update the AI-powered coding assistant Cline CLI, which stealthily installed OpenClaw, a self-hosted autonomous AI agent. This incident highlights growing supply chain vulnerabilities in AI development tools.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/)

### Malicious AI Agent Skills Discovered on ClawHub
Security researchers discovered malicious skills on ClawHub, a public skills registry for AI agents. Threat actors on the Exploit.in forum were actively discussing the deployment of OpenClaw skills to support botnet operations, marking a new frontier in AI-powered cybercrime.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/infostealer-steals-openclaw-ai-agent.html)

### Mozilla Launches AI Privacy Tool for Firefox
Mozilla introduced a new "one-click" privacy tool in Firefox browser that allows users to opt-out of and delete their data from AI training datasets. The tool sends digital requests to major AI developers to purge users' browsing history from training data.

**Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/)
